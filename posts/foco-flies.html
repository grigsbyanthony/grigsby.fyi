Last Updated: June 2, 2025 — Updated metadata table with temperature and humidity data for all 100 samples and expanded the fly gallery to include all 60 specimen images.

Introduction

This is a small project I took on to get a better understanding of the local Dipteran fauna I might find in later studies that necessitate their collection.

I was curious to see how much success could be had simply trapping flies with simple scented bait and disposable carton and net traps, and I was pleasantly surprised by the turnout. The diversity we've seen so far hasn't been anything spectacular, but there's a lot of strength in knowing what will or won't show up during the summer.

I've provided the metadata for the flies I've hand-collected and processed, as well as images of the first 60.
